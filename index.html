This song is perfect for this stage.

I believe is an extremity stemming from this stage.

--Stage Explanation--
This song highlights the inequality
issues in america, and explains how people,

--Applied Term--
Social Contract ( Stage 5 )

--Stage Explanation--
This stage of moral development
is the stage in which people
convert from following the rules
blindly (like in stage 4) to following
rules that benefit society as a whole.
This song paved the way for African-American
artists to speak up about racism
and police brutality in a way no song
had ever done - straight to the throat.
It immediately hit the billboards and
became one of the most revered rap songs
in the game, and helped N. W. A. build their career.
The connection I'm making here is that N. W. A.
was the path that people could
take to decreasing racism against
African Americans, succesfully improving society.
Play  F**k the Police  by N. W. A. (Yes, It's censored)

Catharsis

--Stage Explanation--
Catharsis is defined as a release of emotions and admission
of your true feelings. The first thing I thought of once I heard
this was the admission of love. That's why I chose "closer" by
the chainsmokers; it was the first and best song I thought of
that described love. It's about that type of unconditional love
that still carries on, and meeting that person again and feeling
the same way you did all those years ago.

--Applied Term--
Learned Helplessness

--Stage Explanation--
Learned Helplessness refers to the point in which you
decide that pretty much everything you can do to affect
a situation cannot and will not affect the end result.
Basically, you admit that you are helpless to stop the result
and that the end is unstoppable. The first thing I thought of
was, you guessed it, love. We all know we can't stop loving that
one person sometimes, no matter how hard we try. This song illustrates
that perfectly.
Play  The One That Got Away  by Brielle Von Hugel
