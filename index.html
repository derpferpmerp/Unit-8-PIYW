Good Boy and Good Girl ( Stage 3 )

--Stage Explanation--
This song is perfect for this stage.
It came to mind instantly, and I feel like it's great for a few reasons.
The song is written about a girl, and how all
bruno wanted was for her to show her love
to him, but all he was met with was a cold
hearted girl. He explains how he would butcher
himself in the most gruesome ways just to get her
to love him, even though he knows she wouldn't
do the same for him. He's ready to give up his
life just for her sign of approval, which
I believe is an extremity stemming from this stage.

Law and Order Morality ( Stage 4 )

--Stage Explanation--
This song highlights the inequality
issues in america, and explains how people,
depending on race, face alternate consequences
for the same actions. It shows how they

grave consequences. Although the
original belief of the stage is that this

it still displays the same message.

--Associated Stage--
Social Contract ( Stage 5 )

--Stage Explanation--
This stage of moral development
is the stage in which people
convert from following the rules
blindly (like in stage 4) to following
rules that benefit society as a whole.
This song paved the way for African-American
artists to speak up about racism
and police brutality in a way no song
had ever done - straight to the throat.
It immediately hit the billboards and
became one of the most revered rap songs
in the game, and helped N. W. A. build their career.
The connection I'm making here is that N. W. A.
was the path that people could
take to decreasing racism against
African Americans, succesfully improving society.
Play  F**k the Police  by N. W. A. (Yes, It's censored)

--Associated Stage--
Universal Principles / Self-Determined Rules ( Stage 6 )

--Stage Explanation--
I'd say that Lose Yourself fits this
last stage for a few reasons, but it might
seem like a stretch at the beginning.
Eminem's life story, illustrated by his movie 8 Mile,
is a pretty bleak one, that ends up with
a somewhat-happy ending. Rags to Riches, so to speak.
This song shows how he was really treated back
when he was a kid and was just trying to survive.
As most of us know, Eminem got in his share
of fights when he was younger.
(If you didn't know this, you should watch 8 mile).
Basically, he had to break some rules to
get to where he is today, but none of them
were morally incorrect. Sometimes, in order to
succeed the most in life, you have to bend
the rules. Which is the exact underlying message
of the sixth and final stage from kohlberg's model.
Play  Lose Yourself  by Eminem
